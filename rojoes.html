Rojões à Moda de Vila do Conde

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds pork shoulder, cut into cubes
  • 1/4 cup lard or cooking oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon sweet paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup white wine
  • Chopped fresh parsley for garnish

Preparation

  1. Marinate the Pork:
    In a bowl, combine the pork cubes with minced garlic, sweet paprika, ground cumin, dried oregano, salt, and black pepper. Allow it to marinate for at least 2 hours or overnight in the refrigerator.
  2. Sear the Pork:
    In a large pan, heat lard or cooking oil over medium-high heat. Add the marinated pork cubes and sear them until golden brown on all sides.
  3. Add Aromatics:
    Stir in the chopped onion and bay leaf. Cook until the onion is soft and translucent.
  4. Deglaze with Wine:
    Pour in the white wine to deglaze the pan, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom. Allow the wine to reduce slightly.
  5. Slow Cook:
    Reduce the heat to low, cover the pan, and let the pork simmer for about 1 to 1.5 hours or until the meat is tender and cooked through. Add more wine or water if needed to keep it moist.
  6. Garnish and Serve:
    Before serving, garnish the Rojões with chopped fresh parsley. Serve hot with your choice of side dishes.
